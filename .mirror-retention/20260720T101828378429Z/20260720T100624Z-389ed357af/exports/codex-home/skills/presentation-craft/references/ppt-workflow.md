# PPT Workflow

## Deck Types

- `答辩版`: 11-12 slides, one point per slide, strong transitions, light text.
- `汇报版`: 6-10 slides, summary-first, fewer examples, more decisions.
- `讲稿版`: keep more detail, but still split long paragraphs into readable
  blocks.

## Slide Patterns

- Cover: title, subtitle, presenter, date.
- Problem / background: why the topic matters.
- Mechanism: flow diagram or chain.
- Case slides: one disease or one example per slide.
- Comparison slide: side-by-side table or dual-column layout.
- Progress slide: diagnosis / therapy / delivery / regulation.
- Conclusion: three takeaways, one closing sentence.

## Rewrite Rules

- Cut repeated phrasing first.
- Preserve definitions, mechanisms, numbers, and time-sensitive claims.
- Turn dense paragraphs into 3 to 5 bullets or a flow.
- Prefer nouns and verbs over filler adjectives.
- Move secondary examples to appendix slides if the deck becomes crowded.

## Layout Rules

- Use one dominant visual style per deck.
- Keep a consistent title placement and footer treatment.
- Leave enough whitespace for reading distance.
- Use tables for comparison, not stacked bullet walls.

## Review Checklist

- Slide count matches the audience.
- Each slide has one central claim.
- No slide feels text-heavy when read aloud.
- Claims with freshness risk are easy to trace.
- The deck still contains the content the user asked to keep.
